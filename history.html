History of BUCC

Some notes from ex-members.

Ian Dixson (BUCC ’81-’82)

Now you're asking! I started caving whilst at Derby (what was Derby Lonsdale College) during my initial degree years from 78 to 81. We had a walking weekend in the Mendips, someone dropped out of the parallel caving group so I gave it a go, got hooked and then spent many weekends underground. On moving to Bangor, for my PGCE, there was no CC so set about setting up a new one via the Athletic Union. I honestly can't remember my title....Chair, maybe? Possibly President. There was enough interest during Freshers to form a club aided by a friend from Derby days. We used to run trips to S Wales, Derbyshire, Yorkshire and, during a very cold winter of 81/82, to Ireland. I managed to obtain a very generous Union grant of about £600 to purchase all manner of kit..add a nought on the end for inflation. We must have held formal meetings/had a constitution of some sort/made financial reports but all that is well in the past. The CC carried on for several years after I left. Unfortunately, there was one death I've heard about since...another Ian who tried to free climb a waterfall, fell, was knocked unconscious and drowned. I'm guessing the CC lost members and/or was closed after that. The kit was kept at St Mary's in a kind of undercroft. So, you're best bet might be to go through the Athletic Union as they should have exemplar constitutions. Nowadays, organising caving trips may well come with a lot more in the way of risk assessment/liability insurance and paperwork. We used to get in a minibus, stay at SWCC or a caravan at Ingleton (80p a night each!) and get on with it sometimes having three groups underground in different caves at any one time. I subsequently spent most of my working life teaching geography and organising all manner of school trips in UK and beyond.

Chris Cowdery (BUCC ’93-’96)

BUCC had a Captain during my time there (which was '93 to '96). I am still in touch with a few people from BUCC, and we have an annual caving trip most years. Other random thoughts are:

  1. We used to do a trip to Poachers (Ogof Hen Ffynhonnau) during Freshers Week.
  2. We used to meet every other Monday (I think, it might have been every Monday) at the Friddoed Bar
  3. We used to stay at peoples houses as well as Caving Club huts. We had somebody in Bridgend, Keighley, Trowbridge.
  4. We'd do mines as well, which is handy in North Wales!
  5. We visited Box Mine and camped underground, and did the same in Rhosydd Slate Quarry.
  6. We issued membership cards. I have attached one for reference. The creature was drawn by Graham Tattersall, and was called Stig. We had a colour scheme too, magenta, cyan and yellow, for membership classes (commitee, normal and honourary), but I forget which colour was which.
  7. We used to publish a membership list (probably illegal now), which shows Captain, Treasurer, Secretary, Gear Warden, Safety Officer, Social Secretary and Driver. It also listed the normal members.
  8. We had a number of sets of SRT gear. I remember getting a grant and buying them. I think they went to the AU when the club folded.
  9. I know two people who stayed on for post-graduate studies (one to do a PhD), so at least one of them was in BUCC to the turn of the millennium.
